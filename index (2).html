The Premise
Belinda Ayer, a disgraced network executive, takes over WPBJ, a venerable public broadcasting station. She assumed public TV would be easy. She was wrong...about everything.
12 Episodes
Season 1
30 Minutes
Format

PB&J is a sharp workplace comedy set inside WPBJ, a venerable public broadcasting station in New York City. Think 30 Rock — but instead of NBC, we're behind the cameras at PBS, where the mission is noble, the budget is catastrophic, and the people are anything but.

Belinda Ayer — a ruthless, brilliant, Puerto Rican network executive — gets fired from a major commercial network after a historic ratings collapse. With her options exhausted and her marriage quietly fraying, she takes the only job available: running WPBJ. She assumes it'll be a breather. She's wrong.

What follows is a season of funding crises, a pyromaniac Italian chef, bickering news anchors, a children's show mascot on the verge of a breakdown, and the very foreign concept of begging the public for money. PB&J asks: what happens when flawed, funny people try — desperately — to do good?

The series explores falling from grace and finding redemption in the most unlikely place imaginable.

WPBJ Programming

Shows Within the Show

Children's Programming
Sunflower Street
WPBJ's beloved morning program hosted by Big Seed and his cast of costumed friends. It's sweet, educational, and its costume cleaning bills are WPBJ's second-largest expense.
Cooking / Lifestyle
Teresa's Italian Table
Named after her 3-star Michelin restaurant (now shuttered). Features only imported Italian ingredients, epic meltdowns, and a $1,500 truffle delivery that arrives in Episode 1.
News
Macbain-Ferrer Newsworthy Hour
A steadily fading institution anchored by a man who wants to retire and his protégé who is maneuvering to replace him — while both pretend to be colleagues.
Period Drama (British)
Mahogany Lust
PBJ-UK/Europe's cash cow. A colonial drama about sex, war, and deforestation in 19th-century British Honduras. Wildly popular. Currently the subject of a Belize government lawsuit.
Late Addition · Season 1
Antiques Must Go Show
Belinda's emergency revenue scheme: a live antiques auction from the Goldrock-Steinfeller family mansion. Den Withers' greatest comeback. Raises nearly $3M.
PBJ-UK/Europe · Proposed
Teresa's Indian Table
Belinda's second revenue idea: Teresa cooks Indian cuisine for the British channel. Jeremy is enthusiastic about the concept and horrified about working with his ex-wife.
Season One

Episode Guide

01
La Diabla (Pilot)
Written & Available
Belinda Ayer arrives at WPBJ expecting a quiet soft landing. Instead, she's declared a war criminal by a roomful of kindergartners, threatened with a knife by the station chef, and informed by the PBS VP that the place is essentially broke — all before lunch. By evening she's sweating through her first pledge drive, barefoot on live television, calling the Child Ambassador a "little shit" and demanding ten million dollars. Welcome to public broadcasting.
02
Kindercutter
Developed
Macbain, Ferrer, and Big Seed attempt to unite against Belinda's cuts — and immediately fail to agree on anything. Lacy Macbain senses trouble. Ferrer bribes a cameraman for more face time.
03
Personal Tastes
Developed
Teresa's meltdown upon seeing "ham from Virginia" on the revised food list is historic. Major Donor Astor Goldrock-Steinfeller visits and finds Belinda deeply unimpressive. Jeremy returns from Belize with a scheme and a bill for his villa.
04
Ain't Too Proud to Beg
Developed
Thousands of profanity-laced letters arrive from children worried about Mr. Snowpea. Macbain generously suggests Ferrer cover international stories — on location. Big Seed wants to bring Sunflower Street kids into Teresa's kitchen. Belinda learns the truth about Major Donor's finances from Fiona.
05
Fire and Music
Developed
Shirley's volunteer army descends. Ferrer reports from a Rio favela and picks a live on-air fight with Macbain. Sunflower Street kids reject Teresa's pizza during the pledge drive. Teresa doesn't react well. She and Big Seed come to blows. Suze Orman drops by unannounced.
06
Choice Words
Developed
The emergency pledge campaign is in full swing. Volunteers answer phones, sip mini vodka bottles, and flirt with callers. Fiona's nephew wants to meet Big Seed. Jeremy gives brutally honest feedback honoring Den's documentary legacy.
07
Freedom Clings
Developed
Fiona meets Belinda in jail. Shirley seizes control of WPBJ by virtue of her unpaid status. Big Seed and Mr. Snowpea parade through the halls. Belinda returns to find Shirley unwilling to step down. A kidnapping misunderstanding is finally cleared up.
08
Gifts That Keep on Taking
Developed
Ferrer does a kidnapping story from Colombia and uses Belinda as the lead. Major Donor's mother dies, leaving WPBJ her mansion. Everyone is angry. Den proposes a documentary. During dinner, Fiona mentions the antiques. Belinda calls Den with an idea.
09
Civil Unrest
Developed
The mansion documentary becomes the Antiques Must Go Show. A nature filmmaker appears on Sunflower Street and demonstrates how to shoot pigeons. Ferrer sneaks into a gubernatorial debate to heckle Macbain. Lacy Macbain is waiting for him.
10
Hot Items
Developed
Teresa's cigarettes are defunded. Den is caught tagging antiques with price stickers. The Macbain-Ferrer feud sends ratings through the roof. Belinda replaces PBS mugs and T-shirts with PB&J-branded condoms during a human reproduction documentary.
11
Foreign Substances
Developed
After an inspiring meal at an Indian restaurant, Belinda launches Teresa's Indian Table on P-UKE. Jeremy is thrilled by the concept, horrified by his co-executive producer: his ex-wife Teresa. Fiona's NGO calls for a Mahogany Lust boycott over deforestation.
12
Loot
Developed
The Antiques Must Go Show raises nearly $3 million and attracts thousands of new members. Belinda tells Den to find another mansion and asks him to check obituaries. Sunflower Street films on location in a neighborhood where the kids have their own ideas about enrichment. Teresa submits a $95 receipt for one ounce of an exotic Indian spice.

Raheem Cash
Writer · Creator

Raheem Cash is the writer and creator of PB&J, an original half-hour comedy that he has developed from the ground up — including a full pilot script and a twelve-episode season bible.

PB&J draws on a deep familiarity with the rhythms of workplace comedy, the texture of public media, and the specific comedic pressure that comes from watching overly serious people fail in completely human ways. The show is built to run — with a fully developed ensemble, clear episode arcs, and a season-long narrative engine.

Raheem is seeking representation and production partners who see the opportunity in smart, character-driven half-hour comedy with a genuinely original world.
