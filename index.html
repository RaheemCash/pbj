She assumed public TV would be easy. She was wrong...about everything. 12 EPISODES SEASON 1 30 MINUTES FORMAT PB&J is a sharp workplace comedy set inside WPBJ, a venerable public broadcasting station in New York City. Think 30 Rock — but instead of NBC, we're behind the cameras at PBS, where the mission is noble, the budget is catastrophic, and the people are anything but. Belinda Ayer — a ruthless, brilliant, Puerto Rican network executive — gets fired from a major commercial network after a historic ratings collapse. With her options exhausted and her marriage quietly fraying, she takes the only job available: running WPBJ. She assumes it'll be a breather. She's wrong. What follows is a season of funding crises, a pyromaniac Italian chef, bickering news anchors, a children's show mascot on the verge of a breakdown, and the very foreign concept of begging the public for money. PB&J asks: what happens when flawed, funny people try — desperately — to do good? The series explores falling from grace and finding redemption in the most unlikely place imaginable. He's used to getting his way. RECURRING Den Withers 63 · DOCUMENTARY FILMMAKER (FORMERLY ACCLAIMED) Dozens of acclaimed documentaries. Dozens of years ago. He carries the awards everywhere to remind you. SEASON ONE Episode Guide 01 La Diabla (Pilot) WRITTEN & AVAILABLE Belinda Ayer arrives at WPBJ expecting a quiet soft landing. Instead, she's declared a war criminal by a roomful of kindergartners, threatened with a knife by the station chef, and informed by the PBS VP that the place is essentially broke — all before lunch. By evening she's sweating through her first pledge drive, barefoot on live television, calling the Child Ambassador a "little shit" and demanding ten million dollars. Welcome to public broadcasting. 02 Kindercutter DEVELOPED Macbain, Ferrer, and Big Seed attempt to unite against Belinda's cuts — and immediately fail to agree on anything. Lacy Macbain senses trouble. Ferrer bribes a cameraman for more face time. 03 Personal Tastes DEVELOPED Teresa's meltdown upon seeing "ham from Virginia" on the revised food list is historic. Major Donor Astor Goldrock-Steinfeller visits and finds Belinda deeply unimpressive. Jeremy returns from Belize with a scheme and a bill for his villa. 04 Ain't Too Proud to Beg DEVELOPED Thousands of profanity-laced letters arrive from children worried about Mr. Snowpea. Macbain generously suggests Ferrer cover international stories — on location. Big Seed wants to bring Sunflower Street kids into Teresa's kitchen. Belinda learns the truth about Major Donor's finances from Fiona. 05 Fire and Music DEVELOPED Shirley's volunteer army descends. Ferrer reports from a Rio favela and picks a live on-air fight with Macbain. Sunflower Street kids reject Teresa's pizza during the pledge drive. Teresa doesn't react well. She and Big Seed come to blows. Suze Orman drops by unannounced. 06 Choice Words DEVELOPED The emergency pledge campaign is in full swing. Volunteers answer phones, sip mini vodka bottles, and flirt with callers. Fiona's nephew wants to meet Big Seed. Jeremy gives brutally honest feedback honoring Den's documentary legacy. 07 Freedom Clings DEVELOPED Fiona meets Belinda in jail. Shirley seizes control of WPBJ by virtue of her unpaid status. Big Seed and Mr. Snowpea parade through the halls. Belinda returns to find Shirley unwilling to step down. A kidnapping misunderstanding is finally cleared up. 08 Gifts That Keep on Taking DEVELOPED Ferrer does a kidnapping story from Colombia and uses Belinda as the lead. Major Donor's mother dies, leaving WPBJ her mansion. Everyone is angry. Den proposes a documentary. During dinner, Fiona mentions the antiques. Belinda calls Den with an idea. 09 Civil Unrest DEVELOPED The mansion documentary becomes the Antiques Must Go Show. A nature filmmaker appears on Sunflower Street and demonstrates how to shoot pigeons. Ferrer sneaks into a gubernatorial debate to heckle Macbain. Lacy Macbain is waiting for him. 10 Hot Items DEVELOPED Teresa's cigarettes are defunded. Den is caught tagging antiques with price stickers. The Macbain-Ferrer feud sends ratings through the roof. Belinda replaces PBS mugs and T-shirts with PB&J-branded condoms during a human reproduction documentary. 11 Foreign Substances DEVELOPED After an inspiring meal at an Indian restaurant, Belinda launches Teresa's Indian Table on P-UKE. Jeremy is thrilled by the concept, horrified by his co-executive producer: his ex-wife Teresa. Fiona's NGO calls for a Mahogany Lust boycott over deforestation. 12 Loot DEVELOPED The Antiques Must Go Show raises nearly $3 million and attracts thousands of new members. Belinda tells Den to find another mansion and asks him to check obituaries. Sunflower Street films on location in a neighborhood where the kids have their own ideas about enrichment. Teresa submits a $95 receipt for one ounce of an exotic Indian spice.
